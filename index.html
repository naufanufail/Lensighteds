The Diagnostic Gap

Beyond the Snellen Chart

The Snellen chart has been standard for 160+ years — but it measures the wrong thing for cataract. Crystallin protein aggregation scatters light, degrading contrast and introducing glare long before letter resolution declines.

LENSIGHT captures four functional parameters that Snellen cannot detect: contrast sensitivity, glare sensitivity, detail recognition, and fog perception.

Clinical Science

Scientific Foundation

Contrast

Pelli-Robson (1988)

10-row log contrast array. L = 128×(1−MC). Log CS <1.50 = deficit. Michelson contrast calibration, ±0.05 tolerance. 35% LCRI weight.

Spatial Frequency

Campbell-Robson (1968)

CSF across 1–16 cpd. Cataract scatter preferentially degrades mid–high SF. <4 cpd resolution = functional deficit. 25% LCRI weight.

Glare

C-Quant (van den Berg)

Straylight framework. Retroreflective RA >500 mcd/lux/m². Log straylight >1.2 = clinical deficit. 4 calibrated glare targets. 20% LCRI weight.

Grading

LOCS III Calibration

Lens Opacities Classification System III gold standard. LCRI tier boundaries from regression modelling vs LOCS III grades by fellowship-trained ophthalmologists.

Neuroplasticity

V1 Perceptual Learning

Near-threshold Gabor exposure induces long-term synaptic potentiation in V1 and extrastriate areas. Durable improvements in contrast sensitivity through perceptual learning.

Gabor Function

V1 Simple Cell Activation

Orientation-selective V1 simple cells approximate the Gabor function. Sinusoidal grating × Gaussian envelope. Phase randomised per trial prevents anticipation.
